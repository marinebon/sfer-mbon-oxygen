Cruise WS19322

This report maps the shallowest depth at which the interpolated dissolved oxygen field falls below a chosen threshold (anoxic/hypoxic boundary). Use the slider on the map to change the O₂ threshold; grid cells are colored by depth in meters.

Anoxic depth map

Pan and zoom to explore where oxygen drops below the threshold. Each colored cell shows the shallowest depth (closest to the surface) where interpolated O₂ is below the threshold — not the depth where O₂ equals the threshold. When the threshold exceeds surface oxygen across the domain, expect uniform shallow depths (~2 m, the top of the interpolated grid). Hover a cell for its depth in meters. Use the depth color scale inputs to adjust the color bar limits.
